# Cabo Pulmo National Park History

## Overview

Cabo Pulmo National Park is located in the southern tip of Baja California Sur, Mexico. It is one of the most successful marine protected areas in the world, demonstrating dramatic recovery of fish populations after protection.

## Timeline

### 1995 - Establishment
- Cabo Pulmo National Park officially established
- 7,111 hectares of marine area protected
- Complete no-take zone (no fishing allowed)

### 1998 - LTEM Monitoring Begins
- First systematic monitoring surveys conducted
- Baseline data collection initiated
- Early recovery phase documented

### 2000s - Recovery Period
- Dramatic increases in fish biomass observed
- Large predator populations recovering
- Community support growing

### 2011 - Major Publication
- Aburto-Oropeza et al. publish landmark study in PLoS ONE
- Documents 463% increase in fish biomass since protection
- Global recognition of conservation success

### 2012 - Threat Averted
- Proposed tourism development "Cabo Cortés" cancelled
- Community and scientific opposition successful
- Protected status maintained

### Present - Continued Success
- Biomass continues at high levels
- Model for MPA effectiveness globally
- Ongoing monitoring and research

## Key Findings

### Biomass Recovery
- 463% increase in total fish biomass (1999-2009)
- Top predator populations recovered
- Spillover effects documented in adjacent areas

### Community Involvement
- Local fishers became tour guides
- Ecotourism replaced fishing income
- Strong community support for protection

### Scientific Value
- Long-term monitoring data
- Natural experiment for MPA effectiveness
- Reference site for regional comparisons
