# LTEM Survey Methodology

## Study Design

The Baja California Long-Term Ecological Monitoring (LTEM) program uses a standardized belt transect methodology to survey reef fish communities.

### Sampling Protocol

1. **Transect Design**
   - Belt transects: 50m x 5m = 250 m² per transect
   - 4 transects (replicates) per depth stratum
   - Most reefs have 2 depth strata (Shallow: 5m, Deep: 20m)

2. **Fish Identification**
   - All fish identified to species level
   - Total length estimated (cm)
   - Abundance counted by size class

3. **Survey Timing**
   - Annual surveys (typically August-December)
   - Consistent timing to minimize seasonal effects

## Data Quality

### Strengths
- Long time series (1998-2024)
- Consistent methodology
- Multiple regions covered
- Trained observer teams

### Considerations
- Not all reefs surveyed every year
- Seasonal timing varies slightly
- Observer variability possible

## Regions Covered

1. **La Paz** - Bay of La Paz and surrounding reefs
2. **Corredor** - Corridor between La Paz and Cabo Pulmo
3. **Loreto** - Loreto Bay National Park area
4. **Cabo Pulmo** - Cabo Pulmo National Park (protected since 1995)
5. **La Ventana** - Southern region
6. **Alto Golfo** - Upper Gulf of California
7. **San Basilio** - San Basilio area

## Biomass Calculation

Biomass is calculated using:
- Length-weight relationships (von Bertalanffy parameters)
- Species-specific conversion factors
- Expressed in tons per hectare (ton/ha)

### Formula
```
W = a × L^b
Biomass = Σ(W × N) / Area
```

Where:
- W = individual weight
- L = total length
- a, b = species-specific parameters
- N = number of individuals
