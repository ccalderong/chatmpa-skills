# Impact Classification Guide

## Detailed criteria and difficult cases

This guide complements the skill's controlled vocabulary. Use it when the classification is not obvious.

---

## `Strengthens` — Green

The change makes the plan more specific, more actionable, or better supported than the previous version.

**Clear cases:**
- A numerical target is added where there was none before (e.g., "will be monitored" → "coral coverage will be measured at ≥ 6 sites each spring").
- A responsible institution is named where previously it was generic (e.g., "the authorities" → "CONANP in coordination with the management institution").
- A specific protocol or index is incorporated where there was previously a vague intention.
- A figure is updated with more recent data that better reflects the MPA's reality.
- A new section is added with substantive content that the previous plan did not cover.

**Edge case — Added that does not Strengthen:**
If a new section is added but its content dilutes or contradicts existing commitments (e.g., a permitted-use zone is added that overlaps with a core zone), classify as `Weakens`.

---

## `Neutralizes` — Blue

The change does not alter the plan's commitments, targets, or protocols. It is cosmetic, administrative, or stylistic.

**Clear cases:**
- Spelling or grammar correction without change of meaning.
- Substitution of full name by abbreviation (e.g., "Cabo Pulmo National Park" → "CPNP").
- Reorganization of paragraph order without content change.
- Update of headings or section numbering.
- Change of verb tense without change of commitment (e.g., "will be carried out" → "is carried out").

**Edge case — verbatim reused text:**
If a diagnostic paragraph is carried over unchanged from a version from 10+ years ago, classify as `Neutralizes` (the change itself is null), but in the `Comparison` cell explicitly note that the content was not updated despite the elapsed time — that is valuable information for the human reviewer even if the impact of the *change* is neutral.

---

## `Weakens` — Red

The change reduces the plan's effectiveness, specificity, or ambition relative to the previous version.

**Clear cases:**
- A numerical target is deleted and replaced by a vague formulation (e.g., "recover ≥ 18% coverage by 2030" → "promote coral recovery").
- The mention of a specific responsible institution is deleted.
- A specific protocol or procedure is deleted.
- A commitment is softened (e.g., "prohibited" → "restricted", "will be implemented" → "the possibility of will be evaluated").
- A complete section with substantive content disappears.
- An inconsistency is introduced (contradictory figure, zone that disappears without explanation).

**Edge case — deletion of redundant content:**
If what was deleted was an exact duplicate of another section, classify as `Neutralizes` instead of `Weakens`.

**Edge case — simplification that improves:**
If confusing or contradictory text is deleted and replaced with something clearer and more specific, classify as `Strengthens` even if content was deleted.

---

## Quick-decision table

| Situation | Impact |
|---|---|
| New numerical target or specific indicator | `Strengthens` |
| Numerical target deleted or made vague | `Weakens` |
| Spelling correction / abbreviation / reordering | `Neutralizes` |
| Diagnostic text reused without updating | `Neutralizes` (note in Comparison) |
| Responsible institution named for first time | `Strengthens` |
| Responsible institution deleted | `Weakens` |
| Specific protocol added | `Strengthens` |
| Specific protocol deleted | `Weakens` |
| Figure updated with more recent data | `Strengthens` |
| Contradictory figure without explanation | `Weakens` (→ `Inconsistency`) |
| Zone or subzone that disappears without replacement | `Weakens` (→ `Inconsistency`) |
| New section with substantive content | `Strengthens` |
| Section with substantive content deleted | `Weakens` |
